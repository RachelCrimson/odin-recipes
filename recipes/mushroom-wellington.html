Mushroom Wellington

Description

Potatoes, spinach and a saucy mushroom and onion mix wrapped in a crispy pastry. A hearty, vegetarian and vegan alternative to a beef wellington and a great meat-substitute for holiday dinners. Perfect for serving mutliple guests but also makes delicious leftovers. Best served with a vegetable gravy.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz cremini mushrooms
  • 4 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 white onion
  • 1/4 cup vegetable broth
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp thyme
  • 4 oz spinach
  • 1 sheet of puff pastry
  • potato
  • 1 tbsp milk

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 190°C.
  2. Remove the puff pastry from the fridge to let it soften.
  3. Finely chop the mushrooms and slice the onion.
  4. Peel and slice the potatoes.
  5. Add 2 tbsp of olive oil to a pan and add the onion until translucent.
  6. Add the vegetable broth, garlic, mushrooms, soy sauce, thyme and spinach.
  7. Mix and take off the heat when cooked through.
  8. Carefully unroll the puff pastry onto a baking tray.
  9. On the central third of the puff pastry, place a layer of about 1/3 of the potato slices.
  10. Spread half of your mushroom mix onto the potato layer.
  11. Add another layer of potatoes and then mushroom mix.
  12. Add a final layer of potatoes.
  13. Fold the sides of the puff pastry over the potato layers and pinch the top and bottom of the pastry together to completely incase the filling.
  14. Score the top of the pastry diagonally in both directions.
  15. Top with an 'egg wash' mix of olive oil and milk.
  16. Stab a few holes into the top of the pastry to create air vents.
  17. Place in the oven for 35-40 mins until golden brown.
