Onion Soup

Description

This hearty onion soup recipe is perfect for keeping you warm on cold days. It is an excuse to have your slow cooker running, filling your home with sweet, onion-smelling warmth. The cheesy bread balances the sweetness from the soup creating a rich and delicious combination. An added bonus is that the basic recipe is incredibly affordable! The cheese is the main price factor, meaning you can tailor this recipe to your budget by altering your choice of cheese. Minimal prep is required, the most time-consuming aspect being chopping onions. After that it's a simple case of letting the slow cooker do it's job. Then the most difficult part is waiting, especially with the delicious aromas to tempt you all day!

Ingredients

  • 3 large white onions
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 table spoons brown sugar
  • 64 ounces beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Baguette
  • 3/4 cup shredded Gruyere
  • 1/2 cup shredded Emmental
  • 6 tablespoons shredded Parmesan

Steps

  1. In a large non-stick pan, cook the onions, butter, and brown sugar over medium-low heat until golden and caramelised. (About 20 minutes).
  2. Once golden, add to the slow cooker along with the remaining ingredients except for the bread and cheeses.
  3. Cook on low for 6 to 8 hours.
  4. 15 minutes before serving, preheat your oven to 200°C.
  5. Cut your baguette into slices so that each serving gets two.
  6. Cut a six inch section of your baguette and slice in half longways. Make enough of these halves for one per serving.
  7. Place the bread slices in the oven for a couple minutes until the cheese has melted and the bread has crisped up.
  8. While the bread slices are toasting, remove and discard the bayleaf from the soup before ladling the soup into bowls.
  9. Once the bread slices are done, place two small slices in each bowl and the remaining longer slices on a separate plate.
